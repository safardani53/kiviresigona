Which of the Ladies Is the Richest?

The 'Real Housewives of Atlanta' cast is composed of some financially savvy women. So, what are the net worths of the peach holders?

The fabulous ladies of The Real Housewives of Atlanta have always been vocal about getting to the bag. With the women serving at the helm of beauty brands, fashion endeavors, adult entertainment businesses, and more, the peach holders know a thing or two about keeping the coins rolling in. And after Season 14 and Season 15 welcomed new blood into the mix, fans grew eager to learn more about the ladies.

Season 14 featured Olympic gold-medalist Sanya Richards-Ross and welcomed the return of Shereé Whitfield. The two ladies also returned for Season 15. Kenya Moore, Kandi Burruss, Drew Sidora, and the always fashionable Marlo Hampton are also the focus the current season. And since the ladies continue to make incredible strides in their careers — aside from RHOA — it’s only right we highlight their finances.

We're uncovering the RHOA cast members' net worth and the shocking truth about their pay.

1. Kandi Burruss — $30 million

There's a reason why Kandi "Keep a Check" Burruss has earned her unique moniker. Kandi isn't only a founding member of the R&B super group Xscape, but she's also a songwriter, actor, television host, podcaster, and entrepreneur. Additionally, Kandi is co-owner of the Atlanta hotspot Old Lady Gang, which was showcased on the Bravo franchise Kandi & the Gang. So, it isn't a surprise that Celebrity Net Worth lists Kandi's net worth at $30 million, along with a $450,000 salary per season, as of this writing.

2. Sanya Richards-Ross — $2.5 million

Considering her past and role on the show, we weren't surprised when Jamaican bombshell Sanya Richards-Ross became a Season 14 fan-favorite. Aside from being a breath of fresh air and introducing her Jamaican culture and dialect to the audience, she's quite the accomplished Housewife.

Sanya is a track-and-field athlete who has won not one, not two, but four gold medals. Sanya also works as a TV personality, primarily hosting Olympic coverage, and has also starred in her own reality television show, Sanya's Glam & Gold. Sanya's lengthy résumé has helped her accumulate a net worth of $2.5 million, per Celebrity Net Worth.

3. Drew Sidora — $1.5 million

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Drew has earned a net worth of $1.5 million so far, though some sources speculate she might actually be worth $4 million. The second-time peach holder has made her way in the entertainment industry as an actress and singer. Drew famously starred in the 2006 film Step Up and the hit TV series The Game. Now that Drew is a peach holder, we're sure more opportunities for increasing her net worth will be on the horizon.

6. Marlo Hampton — $600K

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Marlo Hampton is currently working with a net worth of $600,000. This number reflects her work as a reality television star and entrepreneur. And with Marlo's new venture, Le’ Archive LLC, Marlo's net worth is expected to expand.

NeNe Leakes recalls 'getting nothing' for the first few seasons of 'RHOA.'

Although the current cast of RHOA seem to be getting paid quite well for putting some of the more intimate details of their lives on blast, the same can't be said for those who appeared in earlier seasons. In July 2023, NeNe Leakes, an original RHOA cast member, sat down with former RHOA producer Carlos King to discuss RHOA pay.

Article continues below advertisement

NeNe admitted to Carlos, “The first two or three seasons, we weren’t getting nothing, honey.” She also said, “We filmed [Season 1], we knocked it out of the park, and we didn’t get anything." To clarify, the cast did get paid, but it wasn't much. According to NeNe, she only collected $10,000 for Season 1, which took three months to film. By the time Season 2 had rolled around, NeNe says her salary was only bumped up to $50,000.

Fed up with the low pay compared to the high viewer ratings, NeNe says she and other cast members joined forced and demanded more money. By Season 3, she would collect $100,000 (possibly even $150,000), and Season 4 is when the real money came rolling in.
