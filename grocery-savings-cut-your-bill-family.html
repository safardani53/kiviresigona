Mom reveals her savvy 'BORES' money-saving method that cut family's grocery bill from $1,400 to $400

March 12, 2024
blog

GROCERIES are an unavoidable expense.

The average US household spends more than $600 every month on groceries, according to data from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Stores like Aldi and Walmart offer discounted grocery options, but there are ways for shoppers to save no matter what store they prefer.

Becky Guiles, known as Freebielady on TikTok, has shared a video talking about how she trimmed her family's grocery expenses.

The matriarch of a family of four, Becky said she used to spend as much as $1,400 every month on groceries.

Using what she called the "BORES" strategy, Becky said she slashed her monthly bill down to $400.

BORES stands for budget, organize, reduce and reuse, extend, and simplify.

Becky said the system starts with creating a budget, and the subsequent steps should be geared toward meeting that budget.

For her family, she set a $400 monthly budget, aiming to spend $100 per person in the household.

Once you have your price point picked out, you should start organizing a grocery list with that budget and a typical shopping run in mind.

For instance, if you find yourself overstocked on certain products, buy those items less frequently.

On the flip side, if you realize you're picking up some of the same items every week, consider buying those in bulk where possible to save.

The R and the E in BORES, reduce/reuse and extend, go hand in hand.

Limiting portion sizes and eating leftovers can stretch one meal into multiple, and help cut your food spending as a result.

To really reduce and extend your food, you can even try using seeds from the grocery store to build a self-sustaining garden.

The last step in the BORES system is to simplify.

One way to simplify the entire shopping process is to order pickup or delivery, so you're less tempted to stray from your list since nothing will catch your eye on the shelf.

Once you have your groceries for the week, you can also try meal planning and prepping to portion things out in advance.

This will save you the guesswork on whether you'll have enough food for the week, and help cut down on spending on takeout or fast food.

It may take some practice and trial and error, but after a few grocery runs you can master the strategy and utilize it to save some cash.
