Early Career

James Young has played basketball since high school and was honored with ALL-State honors by the Associated Press, Detroit News, and Detroit Free Press. 

Due to his extraordinary skills, he was honored as the McDonald’s All-American in his senior year. 

Young was ranked the No. 3 small forward and the No. 8 player in the nation in 2013 by espn.com.

On October 11, 2012, James joined the University of Kentucky and played collegiate basketball.

During his first year, he was named SEC Freshman of the Week twice, selected for second-team All-SEC honors and SEC All-Freshman team honors, and called the Final Four NCAA All-Tournament team. 

NBA Career

On June 26, 2014, Young was drafted into NBA by the Boston Celtics with the 17th overall pick. 

Unfortunately, he missed out on the summer league because of the injury and spent most of his time in the training camp.

The shooting guard played for the Red Claws at the time to receive consistent playtime. He played 19 games for the Red Claws in his rookie season.

In July 2015, Young was part of the Celtics for the 2015 NBA Summer League and demonstrated fantastic game skills. He was part of 16 games for the Red Claws in the 2015–16 season. 

James joined the Celtics for the 2016 NBA Summer League. In the 2016-17 season, he played 29 games with the club.

After that, he signed with the New Orleans Pelicans for the 2017 NBA Summer League. On September 6, 2017, he joined a training camp contract with the Milwaukee Bucks but was released after one month of signing the agreement on October 5, 2017.

On October 22, 2017, James joined the Wisconsin Herd training camp roster and stayed with the team for a year. 

Young entered into a two-way deal with the Philadelphia 76ers and the Delaware 87ers (the NBA G League affiliate of the 76ers) on January 5, 2018.

On March 26, 2018, he was released from the contract by the 76ers.

In October 2018, Young re-signed with the Wisconsin Herd. He was released after three months by the Herd.

International Career

On August 8, 2019, James Young signed with Maccabi Haifa of the Israeli Premier League. In the 2019-20 season, he became the top scorer of the league. 

Young entered a contract with the New York Knicks on December 14, 2020. Soon, he was released and exchanged to the Westchester Knicks, the Knicks’ G League affiliate. He was with the team for three months.

On March 8, 2021, James joined Hapoel Tel Aviv of the Israeli Premier League and Greek club Kolossos Rodou on January 12, 2023.
