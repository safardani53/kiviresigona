Amateur Career 

Gibson was a basketball star right from his high school days. He was a prominent player at Jones High School in Houston, Texas. 

The player led the school to win the Texas Class 4A state championship, averaging 25.5 points and 9.3 assists per game.

He was a member of the National Honor Society and graduated sixth in high school.

After being a high school standout, Daniel enrolled at the University of Texas and was a standout guard for the Longhorns.

He scored his personal-high 37 points in a game against Kansas State in January 1997. With the Longhorns, Gibson averaged 13.8 points, 3.6 rebounds, and 3.0 assists per game playing 68 games in two seasons.

In his freshman year, he was named All-Big 12 Third Team and became the first freshman in school history to lead Texas in scoring.

Following his sophomore year, he earned Honorable Mention All-America by the Associated Press, First-Team NABC All-District 9 honors, and All-Big 12 THIRD-TEAM selection.

He set a school record for the most three-pointers made in a season (101), and he was awarded the Bob Cousy Award, given to the nation’s top point guard.

Daniel had a fantastic college career, and people were shocked when Gibson revealed his intention to enter the NBA draft, forging his two remaining years of college eligibility.

NBA Career

The Cleveland Cavaliers selected Gibson with the 42nd overall pick in the 2016 NBA draft.

The Cavaliers had the league’s lowest-scoring backcourt in 2006, and adding Gibson to the team was a smart move for the team to stand out in the league.

Gibson led all rookies in three-point field goal percentage with 41.9% and averaged 4.6 points per game in his rookie season.

The Cleveland Cavaliers made it to the playoffs thanks to Daniel’s efforts and soon he was placed in the starting line-up.

Gibson ranked fifth in the NBA in three-point shooting percentage, making 44.0% of his attempts. He was named MVP of the Rookie Challenge and finished second in the Three-point Shootout.

On June 10, 2008, Gibson sustained an ankle injury which forced him to miss several games.

On July 16, 2008, Daniel and the Cavaliers agreed on a five-year/$21 million deal. He scored his 1,000th career point in the Cavs’ game against the Oklahoma City Thunder on November 26, 2008.

It was going good for Daniel in the 2012-13 season until he suffered from another ankle injury that kept him away for two years, and he announced his retirement in 2015.

He capped off his professional career playing 397 games and averaging 7.8 points, 2.0 assists, 0.2 blocks, and 0.6 steals per game.
