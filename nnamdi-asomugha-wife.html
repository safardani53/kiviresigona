About Nnamdi Asomugha

Nnamdi Asomugha was born on July 6, 1981, in Lafayette, Louisiana, the United States, to Nigerian parents.

He grew up in Los Angeles, California.

Asomugha graduated from Narbonne High School in Harbor City, California, and then attended the  University of California, Berkley, where he played for the California Golden Bears football team.

He was drafted 31st overall in the first round of the 2003 NFL Draft by the Oakland Raiders.

Asomugha recorded his first two career interceptions against the Browns in 2006.

On February 19, 2009, he signed a three-year extension with the Raiders, the deal that made him the highest-paid defensive back in NFL history.

He became a free agent entering the 2011 season, and on July 209, 2011 Philadelphia Eagles signed Asomugha to a five-year deal.

After the Eagles failed to resurrect the deal with Asomugha, he signed a one-year contract with the San Francisco 49ers on April 2, 2013.

However, he was waived by the team the same year.

Later that year, he signed a one-day deal with the Raiders so that he could retire as a Raider.

Asomugha hung his boots on December 27, 2013, and left football.

He was named the best Oakland Raiders Player of the Past Decade by Pro Football Focus in 2016.
