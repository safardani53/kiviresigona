Cleaning fanatics are calling 58p Fabulosa spray the new Zoflora

March 11, 2024
blog

IF YOU are having trouble tracking down Mrs Hinch’s favourite Zoflora, we might have another cleaning product for you to try. 

Cleaning fans are hailing the 58p Fabulosa disinfectant as being the “new Zoflora” and saying they want to stock up on the different scents. 

Fans of cleaning influencer Mrs Hinch will recall seeing the Instagram star using diluted Zoflora to clean her picture-perfect Essex home. 

Fabulosa is also a concentrated disinfectant and comes in similar little bottles of varying scents. 

Some of the different options on the website include black cherry, blueberry and cranberry and even “electric unicorn”. 

One woman shared her vast collection of Fabulosa bottles, which she had managed to buy in bulk. 

Writing on the Facebook group Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK, she said: “If you order online the fabulosa site and spend £19.95 you get free delivery. If under you have to pay £5. 

“Some are on sale from 60p!”

People were quick to comment to say how much they loved the disinfectant and even preferred it to Zoflora. 

One said: “these are so good it’s like Zoflora except smells better.”

Another added: “its similar to zoflora disinfect.”

Meanwhile, a fan commented: “like zoflora and smell great.”

The makers of Mrs Hinch’s favourite disinfectant, Zoflora, have revealed how it could help kill coronavirus in your home. 

A Zoflora spokesperson claimed that despite the heavenly-scented spray not being tested on the COVID-19 strain, it could help to kill the virus. 

They told The Mirror: “'The science suggests that Zoflora would be effective against this strain, as it is against other similar viruses within the standard five minutes contact time.”

The popular cleaning product costs around £1 per bottle, and can be purchased from supermarkets and many home stores. 

The spokesperson added: “Like all other disinfectant products, we have been unable to test efficacy against the specific COVID-19 strain of Coronavirus.”
