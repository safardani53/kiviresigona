Flock of 10,000 pheasants on farm in Lancashire to be culled after testing positive for bird flu

March 21, 2024
blog

A FLOCK of 10,000 pheasants on a Lancashire farm are to be culled after testing positive for bird flu.

A number of the farmed breeding birds had already died at the premises in Pilling, Preston, while the remaining live animals will be humanely put down after the outbreak.

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) said a 3km protection zone and 10km surveillance area was in place around the infected farm to limit the disease from spreading.

The H5N8 strain of avian flu was confirmed by the UK's chief veterinary officer.

A full investigation is under way to determine the source of the infection.

Public Health England has said the risk to public health from the virus is "very low", while the Food Standards Agency said bird flu did not pose a food safety risk for UK consumers.

It comes after Defra confirmed a case of bird flu in a flock of about 6,000 turkeys at a farm in East Lindsey, Lincolnshire, on December 16.

Earlier in the month, the strain was also discovered in two small backyard flocks of chickens and ducks on a premises near Settle in North Yorkshire, and Carmarthenshire, south west Wales.

An avian influenza prevention zone was declared on December 6 and will remain in place until February 28.

It requires owners to keep poultry and captive birds indoors or take steps to separate them from wild birds.

The World Health Organisation yesterday called on all countries to closely monitor outbreaks of deadly avian influenza in birds and poultry and to report promptly any human cases that could signal the start of a flu pandemic.
