May 02, 2024

HARRY KANE may not be overjoyed about his new Bayern Munich kit.

Tottenham's all-time top scorer, 30, left North London to join the Bundesliga champions on Saturday in a £104million deal.

But it was not the start he would have wanted as he came off the bench in a 3-0 defeat to RB Leipzig in the German Super Cup.

And it has now come to light that there is a major issue with the white strip which Kane pulled on for his debut.

During the Leipzig loss, it was clear Bayern's away kit was not effectively absorbing heat from the players as their skin was visible underneath.

Footy Headlines have described the jersey as "sticking to their bodies like plastic bags".

They also claim the strip has "poor breathability".

It is believed this was exacerbated by the 80 per cent humidity in Munich on Saturday night.

But it did not go unnoticed by those on social media as one person said: "What are those Bayern shirts made of? Pillows?!

"Everyone is drenched in sweat and they’re totally see-through."

Bayern, who have won 11 Bundesliga titles in a row, get their league campaign underway against Werder Bremen on Friday.

Kane's first home league match then comes against Augsburg on August 27.

At a press conference on Sunday, the striker said: "I spent my whole career and my whole life in England.

"It might take a bit of adapting to get used to a new league, different teams.

"Everyone expects Bayern to walk away with the league every year, but teams are improving and it's a challenge.

"I've adapted throughout my career with Tottenham and the national team, and I'll do that here."
